Four directions

From a quiet colour app to a sheet of paper.

Each screen shows the same note and the same three observations, so the only variable is the interface’s philosophy. They’re ordered by how much they remove.

01 / 04

Graphite

The shipped app, with the colour turned off.

One ink, no accent. Everything the current design says with hue — the pill dot, the six suggestion colours — is re-said with a glyph, a weight, and a word. It’s the least disruptive move here: same layout, same interactions, but nothing on screen competes with the sentence you’re writing. The whole colour budget is spent on your own text selection.

RemovesAll hue. The accent becomes ink.
KeepsSidebar, coach pill, every interaction.
Best forShipping monochrome without a redesign.
EffortLow — a palette swap plus a glyph set.

02 / 04

Page

Warm paper, ink serif, a pencil in the margin.

Two tones — paper and graphite — and a serif set to a real reading measure. The file list becomes a slim contents strip; the coach becomes a copyeditor’s pencil marks, italic and faint, in the right margin next to the line they’re about. Nothing here looks like software. It’s the most opinionated direction and the one that most rewards long-form work.

RemovesColour, cards, chrome — down to two tones.
KeepsAnchored, in-context suggestions.
Best forEssays and arguments you mean to finish.
EffortMedium — margin anchoring, serif tuning.

03 / 04

Spotlight

Only the paragraph you’re in is lit.

Monochrome by mechanism: focus is the interface. The paragraph under your cursor holds full ink; everything else steps back to a quarter-tone. The sidebar shrinks to a spine of dots, the coach to a single count in the corner. You get contrast without ever adding a second colour — the dimming does all the signalling.

RemovesThe sidebar, the coach panel, all hue.
KeepsFull text; it’s dimmed, never hidden.
Best forDrafting when everything else is noise.
EffortMedium — track the active block live.

04 / 04

Teleprompter

No sidebar, no toolbar. One column, centred.

The end of the road. Every frame is gone; there is a measure of text in the middle of the window and one small status glyph at the bottom. Notes, settings, and the coach all live behind keys (⌘K, ⌘.). You don’t manage the app — you just write into it, and summon the rest only when you reach for it.

RemovesEvery persistent control on screen.
KeepsA command palette and the count glyph.
Best forPure flow; deciding what to say next.
EffortLow–medium — mostly things to hide.

The hard part

Six kinds of coaching, zero colours to spend.

Colour is how the app tells a Gap from a Source today. Remove it and the meaning has to travel some other way — never by hue alone (which also fixes the colour-blind case). Each kind gets a geometric glyph drawn from what it means, a fixed word, and a tonal weight. Glyph + word is the signal; the tone is a quiet second channel.

Monochrome kind set

Glyph carries the idea, the label removes all doubt, the ink-weight ranks urgency.
